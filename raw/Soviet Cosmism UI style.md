Describe Soviet Cosmism UI style

---

**Claude**

Soviet Cosmism UI is one of the most distinctive design languages you can pull from — it's deeply philosophical, not just aesthetic. Here's a breakdown:

**Philosophical foundation.** Soviet Cosmism (Fedorov, Tsiolkovsky, Vernadsky) believed humanity's destiny was literal conquest of the cosmos — immortality, resurrection, interplanetary civilization. The UI style inherits that mixture of revolutionary optimism, collectivist scale, and technological mysticism.

**Visual DNA:**

- *Constructivist geometry* — bold diagonal compositions, triangles, circles, radiating lines from a central point. Layout feels like a propaganda poster translated into interface.
- *Futurism meets folk* — industrial machine forms (gears, orbits, sputnik spheres) combined with Slavic ornamental patterns and grain textures.
- *Color palette* — deep Cosmist red (`#C1272D`), golden ochre, Sputnik silver, midnight blue, and the particular orange-white of rocket exhaust. Black is structural, never decorative.
- *Typography* — Cyrillic-inspired geometric letterforms even in Latin text. Wide, spaced, all-caps. No serifs. Text as monumental statement, not navigation.
- *Symbology* — atoms, orbits, cosmonaut silhouettes, launchpads, solar system diagrams, the hand reaching upward. Icons feel embossed on metal.
- *Texture* — aged propaganda-poster grain, linoleum-print texture, lithographic dot patterns. Never photographic.
- *Layout philosophy* — asymmetric but balanced. Grids broken by a single diagonal thrust (a rocket trajectory, a radial beam). Everything has implied momentum.

**UI translation:**

- Dashboards feel like mission control panels — data displayed as gauge clusters, not cards.
- Navigation uses radial or orbital metaphors — hubs and spokes, not sidebars.
- Buttons feel stamped, not clicked — raised metal aesthetic, blocky corners.
- Progress indicators use orbit animations or countdown aesthetics.
- Borders are functional, structural, telegraph-relay thick.

**The key tension** that makes it compelling: it's simultaneously utopian warmth (humanity united, reaching for stars) and cold machine logic (everything is a system, a calculation, a five-year plan). Good Soviet Cosmism UI holds both at once.
