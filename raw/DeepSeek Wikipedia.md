# DeepSeek

**Hangzhou DeepSeek Artificial Intelligence Basic Technology Research Co., Ltd.**, doing business as **DeepSeek**, is a Chinese artificial intelligence (AI) company that develops large language models (LLMs). Based in Hangzhou, Zhejiang, DeepSeek is owned and funded by **High-Flyer**, a Chinese hedge fund. DeepSeek was founded in July 2023 by **Liang Wenfeng**, the co-founder of High-Flyer, who also serves as the CEO for both companies.

The company gained significant international attention in early 2025 with the release of its **DeepSeek-R1** model, which provided performance comparable to top-tier models like OpenAI's GPT-4 and o1, but at a significantly lower training cost.

## History

### Founding and Early Years (2016–2023)
In February 2016, High-Flyer was co-founded by Liang Wenfeng. The company initially focused on using AI for stock trading. By 2021, the firm was using AI exclusively for its trading algorithms, often utilizing Nvidia chips.

In April 2023, High-Flyer announced the launch of an artificial general intelligence (AGI) research lab. Two months later, on July 17, 2023, that lab was spun off into an independent company, **DeepSeek**.

### Model Releases
*   **November 2023:** Released **DeepSeek Coder** and the **DeepSeek-LLM** series.
*   **January 2024:** Released **DeepSeek-MoE** (Mixture of Experts).
*   **May 2024:** Released **DeepSeek-V2**, which introduced Multi-head Latent Attention (MLA).
*   **December 2024:** Released **DeepSeek-V3**, which outperformed many established models while maintaining low training costs (reported at US$6 million).
*   **January 2025:** Launched the **DeepSeek-R1** model and chatbot. The app quickly became the most downloaded freeware app on the iOS App Store in the U.S., surpassing ChatGPT.
*   **April 2026:** Released a preview of the **V4 series**, including the 1.6-trillion parameter DeepSeek-V4-Pro.

## Company Operation

DeepSeek is headquartered in Hangzhou and is primarily funded by High-Flyer. CEO Liang Wenfeng holds a majority stake in the company.

### Strategy
DeepSeek focuses heavily on research and has stated it does not have immediate plans for commercialization. This allows it to bypass certain consumer-facing AI regulations in China. The company's hiring strategy emphasizes skills over experience, often recruiting fresh graduates and individuals from non-computer science backgrounds (e.g., poetry or advanced mathematics) to broaden model capabilities.

## Technical Innovations

DeepSeek is known for its "open-weight" models, where parameters are shared but training data is not necessarily open. Key technical contributions include:

*   **Multi-head Latent Attention (MLA):** A mechanism introduced in V2 to reduce KV (key-value) cache size and memory usage.
*   **DeepSeekMoE:** A variant of Mixture of Experts that uses "shared experts" (always activated) and "routed experts" (activated based on scores) to improve efficiency and prevent expert overuse.
*   **Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO):** A reinforcement learning variant used to train reasoning models like DeepSeek-Math and DeepSeek-R1 without requiring a separate model-based reward system.
*   **Efficiency:** The company has successfully trained high-performing models using older or restricted hardware (like Nvidia A100s) by refining algorithms to maximize computational efficiency.

## Significance

DeepSeek's rise has been described as a **"Sputnik moment"** for the U.S. AI industry. Its ability to produce world-class models at a fraction of the cost of Silicon Valley rivals (e.g., $6M for V3 vs. $100M+ for GPT-4) has upended assumptions about the necessity of massive capital and hardware for AI progress. Following the release of R1, several Chinese tech giants (ByteDance, Tencent, Baidu) cut prices for their own AI models, triggering a price war in the region.
