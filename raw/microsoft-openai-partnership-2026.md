The Next Phase of the Microsoft OpenAI Partnership
Date: April 27, 2026

REDMOND, Wash. — Microsoft Corp. and OpenAI today announced a significant amendment to their strategic partnership, reflecting the evolving landscape of artificial intelligence and the growth of both organizations.

Key Changes:

1. End of Exclusivity: The partnership is transitioning to a non-exclusive relationship. OpenAI is now permitted to license its models and products to third parties on any cloud platform, providing OpenAI with greater flexibility to reach a wider range of customers and partners globally.

2. Intellectual Property License: Microsoft retains its non-exclusive license to OpenAI’s current and future intellectual property (including models and products) through 2032. This ensures continued integration of OpenAI’s state-of-the-art technology across Microsoft’s product portfolio, including Copilot and Azure OpenAI Service.

3. Revenue Sharing Adjustments: As part of the restructuring, Microsoft will no longer pay a revenue share to OpenAI. OpenAI’s revenue-share payments to Microsoft will continue through 2030, subject to a newly established total cap.

4. Cloud Partnership: Microsoft remains OpenAI's primary cloud partner. Azure will continue to be the primary platform for training and deploying OpenAI’s models. OpenAI products will continue to be available first on Azure, contingent on Microsoft’s ability to provide the necessary computational capabilities and infrastructure.

Strategic Vision:

This amendment allows both companies to pursue their respective goals while maintaining a strong collaborative foundation. Microsoft continues to invest heavily in its own AI research and development, led by Mustafa Suleyman and the newly formed Microsoft AI division. OpenAI’s transition aligns with its broader structural evolution to attract traditional capital and scale its mission of ensuring AGI benefits all of humanity.

"OpenAI has been a phenomenal partner, and we are excited to continue our work together in this next phase," said Satya Nadella, Chairman and CEO of Microsoft. "This new agreement provides both organizations with the agility needed to lead in the rapidly changing AI era while ensuring Azure remains the best place for developers to build with OpenAI’s groundbreaking models."

Sam Altman, CEO of OpenAI, added: "Microsoft’s support has been instrumental to our progress. This evolution of our partnership allows us to expand our impact by bringing our models to more people and platforms, while deepening our core collaboration with Microsoft on Azure infrastructure and products."

Microsoft remains a significant shareholder in OpenAI and remains committed to the long-term success of the partnership.
