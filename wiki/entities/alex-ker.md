# Alex Ker

Engineer writing about agent harnesses. Twitter handle **@thealexker**; GitHub handle **AlexKer**. The entity page is filed under the GitHub-style name to match `panutat-tejasen` / `nattee-niparnan` conventions.

## What the source attests

- Active user and contributor to open-source harnesses: [Roo Code](https://roocode.com/), [DeepAgent CLI](https://docs.langchain.com/oss/python/deepagents/cli/overview), [[humanlayer|HumanLayer]].
- Former / current work at [[baseten|Baseten]] — built **gpt-oss-swarm** when `gpt-oss-120b` launched (referenced at [github.com/AlexKer/gpt-oss-swarm](https://github.com/AlexKer/gpt-oss-swarm)), a parallel-fan-out subagent prototype.
- Published (2026-04-18) *"Harnesses are everything. Here's how to optimize yours."* — synthesizes harness-optimization advice across progressive disclosure, R.P.I. prompting, and subagent patterns.

## Stance

The harness — not the model — is where engineering judgment lives in AI coding. The comparison he makes: "engineers used to argue about IDEs, now we argue about harnesses." Treats harness as a persistent piece of team infrastructure that accumulates institutional knowledge; switching harnesses resets that.

Draws repeatedly on [[humanlayer|HumanLayer]] (Kyle) for framings: the "dumb zone" around instruction budget, the R.P.I. framework.
