# The Next Phase of the Microsoft OpenAI Partnership

On April 27, 2026, Microsoft and OpenAI announced a major restructuring of their partnership, moving from an exclusive relationship to an "open relationship" that provides both companies with more strategic flexibility.

## Key Takeaways

### 1. End of Exclusivity
- OpenAI is now free to license its models and products to third parties on any cloud platform (e.g., Google Cloud, AWS).
- This is a significant shift from the previous exclusive arrangement where Microsoft was the sole cloud provider for OpenAI's commercial licensing.

### 2. Intellectual Property & Licensing
- Microsoft retains a **non-exclusive license** to OpenAI’s IP (including all current and future models) through **2032**.
- This ensures Microsoft can continue to integrate GPT-5.5 and future frontier models into Azure and Copilot.

### 3. Financial Restructuring
- **Revenue Share**: Microsoft will no longer pay a revenue share to OpenAI.
- **OpenAI Payments**: OpenAI's revenue-share payments to Microsoft continue through 2030 but are now subject to a **total cap**.
- Microsoft remains a major shareholder ($225B valuation).

### 4. Cloud Priority
- Microsoft (Azure) remains the **primary cloud partner**.
- OpenAI products will continue to ship **first on Azure**, provided Microsoft can meet the required scale and infrastructure needs.

### 5. Strategic Independence
- The move signals Microsoft's push for AI independence, following the appointment of **Mustafa Suleyman** to lead the new "Microsoft AI" division and their development of home-grown models.
- OpenAI's for-profit arm is also restructuring to attract traditional capital more easily.
