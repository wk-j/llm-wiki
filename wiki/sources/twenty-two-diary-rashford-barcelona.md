# What Happened to Rashford... Before Deciding to Move to Barcelona?

A video essay by **[[twenty-two-diary|TwentyTwo Diary]]** exploring the decline and eventual departure of Marcus Rashford from Manchester United to FC Barcelona (set in a 2025/2026 timeline). The source analyzes the psychological and professional factors that led to his exit from his boyhood club.

## Key Takeaways

### 1. The Burden of Early Success
- Rashford broke into the Manchester United first team at 18, scoring 8 goals in his first 6 games.
- Early comparisons to legends like Wayne Rooney and Cristiano Ronaldo created immense pressure that became difficult to sustain.
- His career is described as a series of peaks and deep troughs, rather than a consistent upward trajectory.

### 2. Psychological Impact and Public Scrutiny
- The Euro 2020 final penalty miss and subsequent racial abuse were identified as a critical turning point.
- Unlike teammates who recovered quickly, Rashford appeared to struggle deeply with the hate and loss of confidence.
- His off-field charity work, while heroic, sometimes became a target for critics when his on-field performance dipped.

### 3. Professional Decline at Manchester United
- Under Erik ten Hag, Rashford had a 30-goal season (2022/23), but his performance collapsed shortly after signing a massive £370k/week contract.
- His relationship with the club and fans deteriorated due to disciplinary issues (missing training after a night out) and perceived lack of effort.
- By 2025, he was seen as an "unsellable asset" due to high wages and low output, leading to a free loan move to Barcelona.

### 4. The Move to FC Barcelona
- Barcelona, facing financial constraints, took Rashford on loan as a "low-risk, high-reward" gamble.
- For Rashford, the move represents a "new life" and a chance to escape the intense pressure of being the face of Manchester United.
- The success of the move depends on his ability to regain his standard and adapt to a new league at what should be his physical peak.
