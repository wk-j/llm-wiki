# วิกฤตภาษาเอไอ (AI Language Crisis Phenomenon)

**Source:** Thairath Plus (May 3, 2026)

This article explores the phenomenon of "AI language" (often referred to as Text Slop or AI Tells) that is flooding the internet. It highlights how over-reliance on AI for writing leads to hollow, repetitive, and cliché-ridden text, which could eventually restrict the depth and diversity of human language.

## Key Takeaways

- **The Anatomy of Text Slop:** AI-generated writing frequently relies on highly repetitive patterns and clichés. Common "AI tells" include:
  - The "Not just X, but Y" (ไม่ใช่แค่... แต่คือ...) formula.
  - Overused buzzwords like "delve", "unlock", "empower", "elevate", and "crucial".
  - Grandiose openings (e.g., "In today's fast-paced digital world...").
  - Unnecessary groupings of three (always listing three examples even when one or two suffice).
  - The overuse of em-dashes (—) to manufacture artificial dramatic tension.
- **Why AI Writes This Way:** LLMs process vast amounts of text to find the most probable next word, effectively averaging out language. This flattens nuance, removes true emotional depth, and strips away genuine contradictions, resulting in a "safe," neutral, and highly predictable output.
- **The Threat of Language Collapse:** As the internet fills with AI text, future models will train on this synthetic, averaged data. Over time, human language risks becoming less flexible and less expressive, leading to a broader "language crisis."
- **Co-Creation over Replacement:** The author advocates for treating AI strictly as a tool for acceleration (Co-Creation), not as a wholesale replacement for human writers. Reading and writing must remain human endeavors to preserve emotion, value, and true intent in our communication.
